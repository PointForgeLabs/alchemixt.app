Cocktail Intelligence

Built in New Orleans, for people who
take the craft seriously.

A working tool for bartenders. A menu development platform for bars. A private deployment offering for the spirits brands and hospitality groups building what comes next.

Alchemixt is a cocktail tool I built in New Orleans over the last two years. At its core is a flavor engine that scores about 1,400 ingredients across aromatic and taste profiles, and uses that data to generate riffs, full menus, substitution logic, and training references that a bar team can actually work from.

I built it because the tool I wanted didn't exist, and because I got tired of watching good bar programs pay consultants for work that should have compounded instead of evaporating after every quarterly refresh. What started as a personal project is now three things: a working app for bartenders, a professional platform for bars and restaurant groups, and a private deployment offering for the companies that sit above the bar in the cocktail industry.

Who Built This

Bootstrapped in New Orleans. Built by hand.

I'm Jason, the founder. Alchemixt is built in New Orleans and it is bootstrapped. I spent a decade in institutional finance before this, and I've bartended private events in New Orleans on the side for a while. I built Alchemixt because the tool I wanted didn't exist, and because no one I talked to in the cocktail world had one that did.

The version of this project that took venture money, scaled fast, and chased consumer downloads would look completely different. It would probably also be worse. I'm taking a slower path because the craft this product serves is one I care about, and because I'd rather build something that lasts than something that trends. If that sounds like the kind of company you want to work with, any of the three doors above will take you somewhere useful.
