The Tasting Panel

Nine bartenders.
One craft.

A small, deliberately curated group of bartenders helping shape Alchemixt from the inside. The inaugural panel will be announced at launch.

A cocktail tool built by one person is, honestly, just an app. A tool shaped and endorsed by a small group of bartenders whose craft you respect is something different. It has a point of view, a human lineage, and a set of commitments that software alone can't produce.

The Alchemixt Tasting Panel is how we're making sure Alchemixt has all three. Nine bartenders, carefully chosen for their craft, their range, and their willingness to tell us the honest version of what's working and what isn't. They'll contribute signature specs, technique notes, and seasonal picks. They'll see new features before the rest of the world does. They'll have a real voice in where the product goes next.

And their names will be on it. Because this kind of work deserves credit, not anonymity.

How It Works

A small commitment. A meaningful role.

The panel is designed to respect the time of the people on it. The obligations are intentionally small, because working bartenders are busy and good ones are busier. What the panel asks of its members is bounded and honest.

Quarterly Contribution

One thing, four times a year.

Each panelist contributes once per quarter: a signature spec, a short technique note, a riff on a classic with commentary, or an Ingredient of the Week submission. Panelists pick which one they want to do each time. Most will spend 15 to 30 minutes per quarter on this.

Annual Dinner

One meal, once a year, in New Orleans.

The panel meets once a year for a private dinner in New Orleans. Alchemixt hosts. No agenda beyond gratitude and conversation. The dinner is the part that turns a list of names into an actual community, and it's the one commitment we won't compromise on.

Real Voice in the Product

Consulted, not performative.

Panelists see product changes before anyone else and are asked for feedback on real decisions. When the engine changes, they get notified. When a major feature is being scoped, their input shapes it. This isn't an advisory board that meets quarterly to nod at slides. It's a working relationship.

What Panelists Receive

Access, credit, and a seat at the table.

The panel is compensated in access and community, not cash. Here's what membership includes for the inaugural nine.

  • 01
    Lifetime Industry access A full Alchemixt Industry license for the panelist's bar, free for as long as the panel exists. Unlimited bartenders on the team. Value: $600 per year.
  • 02
    Referral revenue share Twenty percent of year-one revenue on any bar a panelist refers that becomes a paying customer. Paid quarterly. No cap.
  • 03
    Named credit and visibility A dedicated profile on alchemixt.app with photo, bio, and bar affiliation. Every contribution credited prominently. The panel is named in all press coverage.
  • 04
    The annual dinner A private dinner in New Orleans, once a year, hosted by Alchemixt. Part celebration, part editorial planning, part community-building.
  • 05
    Early access to the product New features, engine changes, and partnerships land on the panel before they land anywhere else. Feedback shapes the roadmap before it's public.
  • 06
    A seat at the table As Alchemixt grows, panelists are consulted on major decisions. New categories, new partnerships, new directions. The panel's voice matters beyond its quarterly contributions.
The Inaugural Panel

Announcements are forthcoming.

Recruitment for the inaugural nine is underway. Panelists will be introduced together at launch, with a dedicated page featuring each member's photo, bio, bar affiliation, and first contribution to the platform. Until then, membership is confidential while conversations are in progress.

If you're a bartender or beverage director and someone you trust mentions that your name came up, it probably did. We're being deliberate about who we invite, and we'd rather have nine people we respect than twenty we don't. If the conversation hasn't reached you yet and you think it should, we'd like to hear from you.
